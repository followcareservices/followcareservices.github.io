Why Cultural Safety Matters in Disability Support

For many people from CALD backgrounds, navigating the NDIS can feel overwhelming — especially when services aren't delivered in a culturally familiar way. Language barriers, unfamiliar systems, and workers who don't understand your cultural values can make accessing support feel harder than it should be.

FollowCare was built with this in mind. We believe culturally safe care isn't a bonus feature — it's the foundation of quality support. That means speaking your language, respecting your customs, and matching you with workers who understand your community.

We proudly serve participants from diverse backgrounds across Adelaide's northern, southern, eastern, and western suburbs — communities where CALD populations are largest and NDIS navigation support is most needed.

What Makes FollowCare CALD-Friendly

Professional Interpreter Services

We arrange accredited interpreters at no cost to you — for initial consultations, onboarding, service reviews, and any time you need one. No relying on family members to translate sensitive information.

Aligned with the NDIS CALD Strategy 2024–2028

The NDIS recognises that people from CALD backgrounds face unique barriers to accessing disability support. The NDIS CALD Strategy 2024–2028 commits to making the scheme more accessible, responsive, and culturally safe for all participants. FollowCare is committed to delivering services that align with this strategy — ensuring every person, regardless of cultural background, can access the support they need and deserve.
